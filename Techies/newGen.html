Kimberly Bryant

Founder of Black Girls Code, which introduces girls of color to computer science with the goal of building a new generation of coders. The company aims to train 1 million girls by 2024. Bryant, an electrical engineer from Memphis, attended the Vanderbilt University School of Engineering.

Contact Contribution

Michael Berhan

Founder of POCIT (People of Color in Tech), a resource dedicated to telling the stories of people of color in tech, and host of the Techish podcast. In addition to articles and interviews from individuals in the tech industry, POCIT also hosts a recruitment platform, pocitjobs.com, that helps connect people of color with tech jobs.

Contact Contribution

Saron Yitbarek

Founder of CodeNewbie which offers a wide range of resources, including a Twitter chat, an annual conference, podcasts, and more. Today Saron is building Disco, making technology more accessible through audio courses on subjects like machine learning, cryptocurrency, and ethics in AI.

Contact Contribution

Tony Effik

Managing director at Google, an adjunct professor at Columbia University’s engineering school, and the co-founder of Black and Brilliant which is an advocacy network dedicated to upskilling talented people of color and indigenous people.

Contact Contribution

Timnit Gebru

Computer scientist, advocate for diversity in tech, and co-founder of Black in AI, an initiative geared towards increasing the presence of Black people in the field of artificial intelligence. Black in AI provides mentorship, academic programs, advocacy, events, and community building.

Contact Contribution

Tope Awotona

CEO of Calendly. He launched Calendly using funds from his 401(k), and has been outspoken about the difficulty he faced in raising VC funding. Tope’s story is an inspiring one of overcoming the odds to build a business worth over $50 million in annual revenue without funding.

Contact Contribution

Stacy Brown-Philpot

Chief executive of TaskRabbit and one of the few prominent black women in Silicon Valley. Before TaskRabbit, Stacy was at Google, where she founded the Black Googler Network, an employee resource group.

Contact Contribution

Ismail Ahmed

Founder & Chairman @WorldRemit, a better way to send money. Founder @SahamiyeFdn, an operating foundation helping Somaliland to tackle development challenges

Contact Contribution

Lynsey Scott

Lyndsey is an actor, model, and software Engineer. She had exclusive contracts with Calvin Klein, Prada, Gucci, Louis Vuitton, and Victoria’s Secret. She’s ranked in the top 2% of question answerers on Stack Overflow; and has done video tutorials for kids co-starring the likes of Bill Gates and Mark Zuckerberg for Code.org.

Contact Contribution

Sheena Allen

In 2016, Sheena got the title as the youngest female in America to own and operate a digital bank. The fintech company connects underserved millennials and Gen Z to today’s cashless economy. Sheena is a 2018 Business Insider Under 30 Innovator, part of the 2019 class of Forbes 30 Under 30 and Inc. Female Founders 100 list.
